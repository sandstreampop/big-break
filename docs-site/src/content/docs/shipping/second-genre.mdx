The engine's whole pitch is that a second genre is *content, not surgery*.
**The villa game** — a reality-dating-show season with couplings, recouplings,
Casa Amor, a public vote, and three ways to win — is that pitch, shipped. It
runs on the exact same engine as the music game, and building it touched only
its own new files.

Read this page when you're wondering whether *your* game idea fits: it shows
how far the pack format stretches before you'd ever need to touch shared code
(answer: much further than one genre swap).

## Mapping a TV format onto the engine

Every villa concept found a home on a mechanic the engine already had. This
table is the useful part of the case study — it's how you'd map *your* genre:

| The show wants… | The pack uses… |
|---|---|
| A **season** of six weeks with an early phase, a turn, and a final week | six manifest `segments` — same machinery as the music game's three acts |
| Your **Islander** (a personality type × a gender) | a loadout — the persona pick *is* the character pick |
| **Rizz / Loyalty / Savvy / Charisma**, and being **In Your Head** | manifest stats, plus the built-in attrition slot reskinned |
| The **public's mood** in three factions | manifest resources, with a plugin deriving the overall "Public" score |
| The other couples' simmering **storylines** | a drama plugin: `requires` gates stage the beats, deck weighting brings them forward |
| Declaring your **intention** mid-season | the crossroads — `commitPath` + `winGates`, unchanged |
| **The Connection** with your partner, surviving recouplings | a coupling plugin that owns the `bond` resource and its own verbs |
| **Casa Amor** and the postcard twist | a forced card chain plus flags that a later card detonates |
| Scheduled set-pieces (bombshell arrivals, movie night) | the same scheduled-slot mechanism the music game uses for shops |
| Playing an **angle** for the cameras | the gear mechanic, reskinned — buy it with a resource, lose it on a bad beat |
| Getting **dumped** from the villa | the built-in attrition fail plus pack-declared fail states |
| *"I've got a text!"*, the host, the narrator's asides | presenter copy and styling hooks — pure data |
| Branching **conversation scenes** | card chains: a different follow-up card per choice and outcome |
| Characters with **moods, opinions, and secrets** | plugins with their own state — inventory-style secrets, opinion tracks |

Two loadout quirks even bend *plugin-owned* meters (one type builds the
Connection faster; another gains followers from drama) — the loadout carries
the numbers, the owning plugin reads them, and the engine knows about neither.

## The vocabulary the engine never sees

The villa's verbs — `couple`, `switchPartner`, `reveal`, `gainIntel`,
`deployIntel`, and a dozen more — don't exist in the engine. They're
[declared by the pack's own plugins](/big-break/docs/authoring/effects/), used
by its cards, and validated like any other verb. A music card can't use them;
a villa card can't use the music pack's. Each game's language is its own.

<Aside type="note" title="What this proves for your game">
The minimal probe pack proves the engine boots with almost nothing. The villa
pack proves the opposite end: a real genre with five custom subsystems, a
mid-season set-piece, its own fail state, and a completely different tone —
shipped as new files only. If your idea is a run-based game of loaded choices,
the space between those two is yours to fill without asking anyone's
permission.
</Aside>
