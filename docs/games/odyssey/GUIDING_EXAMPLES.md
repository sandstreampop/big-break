# GUIDING EXAMPLES — The Odyssey, worked scenes

Whole moments rendered end-to-end, so content authors pattern-match against
**scenes**, not isolated lines. Every weather from [`VOICE.md`](./VOICE.md) is
labelled where it speaks. These are *reference*, not shipping cards — but
they're written to pass the lint floor (≤2 `!` and no stacked bangs, no
blocklist cliché or tell in narration, outcomes ≤420 chars, curly quotes).

**Provenance:** [`grill.md`](./grill.md) names this canon set (cold open ·
Act I intro · the Squall · three result tiers · the bard slips · Cyclops
doors · a god notices · the Calypso cash-out · the hollow win · the Despair
fail · the humor layer). The interview transcript itself was not checked into
the repo, so these are authored fresh under the laws — **batch zero of the
verdict loop**, to be confirmed or corrected by human verdicts before the
deck is written against them.

Cards are shown in their engine shape: **Context**, a **Prompt**, choices
(tale-copy labels + the doors they lean on), and outcomes by tier (bad /
good / incredible). Effects sketched in `‹brackets›` show intent, not tuning.

## 1 · The cold open — *the frame*

The bard takes the fire. Warmth first; the audience is won before the ship
sails. Note the performance questions, the fee, the horse gag planted early.

> *(Frame)* Come in, come in — there is room at the fire; there is always
> room, though the man with the cart must leave the cart. The wine goes
> left, friends, and if it reaches me empty I will remember your faces.
> Tonight? Tonight I sing the long way home. Troy is ash behind us. Every
> captain who lived is racing his neighbour for his own harbour — and one of
> them, the one the gods argued about at supper, will take ten years to
> cross what a gull crosses in a week. You know where it ends. You do not
> know how. How is tonight’s business.

---

## 2 · Act I intro — *The Sack and the Sea*

The threshold: the frame hands over to the telling. The roll begins — long
sentences carrying freight, an invented epithet, the first quiet warning.

> *(Frame → Telling)* Twelve ships out of Troy, friends — twelve ships
> riding low, heavy with bronze and with men who had lived, every rower
> pulling for a wife who had learned to run a farm without him. And listen
> how the water was, that first week: easy, foam like combed wool, the kind
> of sea that makes a captain generous at supper. That is the sea’s oldest
> trick. It shows you your harbour in your mind’s eye. Then it asks your
> name.

---

## 3 · The Squall — *a deck card, worked end-to-end*

The connective sea between landmarks. Telling weather throughout: concrete
detail, named crew, the turn in the last clause. All six tiers shown — this
is the pack's three-result-tiers reference.

> **Context:** Act I · open water, three days out from the Cicones
>
> **Prompt** *(Telling)*: The wind backs at dusk — one cold breath out of
> the north, and the sea goes the colour of a bruise. Old Perimedes is
> lashing the jars without being told, which is how you know. The mast
> complains in a voice you have not heard it use before, and the men look at
> you, all of them, at once.

**Choice — left · “Run before it”** ‹Might — trust the hull and the rowers›
- **bad** *(Telling)*: The following sea takes the steering oar out of
  Eurylochos’ hands and swats it flat, like a door slammed in a great house.
  Dawn finds you bailing with helmets, and the morning count comes up two
  benches short. No one says the names loudly. ‹Expedition −, Despair +›
- **good** *(Telling)*: You run all night with the storm grumbling behind
  you like a cheated innkeeper, and at first light the lookout laughs out
  loud — twelve sails, all twelve, strung out and whole on a flat grey sea.
  ‹Expedition held, Might proven›
- **incredible** *(Telling)*: The ship finds the storm’s own stride and
  holds it. Men who were praying at midnight are singing by the middle
  watch, and years from now there will be rowers who claim they pulled an
  oar that night who were never there. ‹Renown +›

**Choice — right · “Give the sea its due”** ‹Lore — heave to, pour the wine›
- **bad** *(Telling)*: The libation goes into the water and the water does
  not care. A long night, hove-to, the hull working under you like a beast
  in a bad dream; by morning the seed-grain jars are brine, and the hungry
  months are laid down like a keel. ‹Expedition −›
- **good** *(Telling)*: You pour the old wine — the good jar — and say the
  sailor’s words your father used. The storm walks past in the dark two
  miles to leeward. You hear it all night, like a war you were not invited
  to. ‹Poseidon soothed›
- **incredible** *(Telling)*: The wine goes into a black sea and the sea
  goes quiet the way a hall goes quiet when a king stands. In the morning a
  following wind sets in from the south-west and stays three days, and not a
  man aboard will speak of it above a whisper. ‹Athena +, Poseidon −›

## 4 · The bard slips — *the frame, mid-telling*

The diegetic skin on the engine's randomness: when the numbers surprise, the
bard is improvising, misremembering, being corrected. The woman by the
woodpile is canon.

> *(Frame)* — six ships. Did I say nine? Six. The woman by the woodpile is
> holding up fingers at me; her grandfather, I am reminded, knew a rower.
> Six ships, and I will thank the fire to remember I said so from the start.

## 5 · The Cyclops — *a landmark, the three-door grammar*

Act I's turning point. The prompt is telling-weather with the dread rising;
the doors are tale-copy (a verb with the door's flavor in it); the violence
line ("I will not sing at this fire") is the canonical worked example.
Afterwards, the name-brag — the beat the Crossroads reads.

> **Context:** Act I landmark · the cave
>
> **Prompt** *(Telling)*: The cave smells of curds, and under that of
> something older and worse. The sheep come home at dusk by themselves, sure
> of the path, and then the light at the cave mouth goes out all at once —
> not a cloud. A stone. A stone the size of your ship, set in the doorway
> the way you would set a cup on a table. Whatever shepherds here has just
> shut you in with it.

**Door — Might · “Put spears to him in his sleep”** ‹requires Might, Expedition high›
- **bad** *(Telling)*: Six of you drive the fire-hard point — and it is
  Polites, hands still on the shaft, who says the thing that stops yours:
  *the stone.* The stone only he can lift. Kill him and the cave keeps you.
  You stop. Above you, the eye opens. ‹Expedition −, Despair +›

**Door — Guile · “Be Nobody”** ‹requires Guile — wine and a false name›
- **good** *(Telling)*: “Nobody,” you tell him, filling the bowl a third
  time. “My father called me Nobody; my mother; all my friends.” And the
  eye, wet and huge as a shield-boss, believes you. Later, howling in the
  dark, he tells his brothers that Nobody is hurting him — and his brothers
  go back to bed, grumbling about the noise. ‹escape opens, Renown +›

**Door — Lore · “Claim the guest-right”** ‹requires Lore — name the law of Zeus›
- **bad** *(Telling)*: You name the gods and the law of the guest, and the
  laugh comes down like rocks going down a hill: the Cyclopes were old, he
  says, before the gods learned their own names. What he did then, I will
  not sing at this fire. The count in the morning was two short. ‹Expedition −›

**The name-brag** *(the beat the Crossroads reads — after the escape)*

> **Prompt** *(Telling)*: The prow is out of stone-throw. Behind you the
> blind giant wades to his waist, feeling for you across the water.
> Eurylochos has your cloak in his fist and his voice is very low. The name
> is in your mouth like a coal.

- **“Swallow it”** ‹Nostos-lean — the sea forgets Nobody› *(Telling)*: You
  say nothing. The men row. The name goes down with the anchor-stone,
  somewhere it can never be shouted from, and the giant is still calling
  *Nobody, Nobody* to the gulls when the island drops under the rim of the
  sea. ‹Poseidon contained›
- **“Shout it at the sea”** ‹Kleos-lean› *(Telling)*: You cup your hands and
  give him the name — whole, father and city and all, so the world will know
  who did this. The men stop rowing. Far under the keel, something that was
  not listening before begins to listen. ‹Renown +, Poseidon +, the scar›

## 6 · A god notices — *the deep*

The hush. No exclamation nine fathoms down; nothing pulses. Dread is quiet
and the sentences shorten of their own weight.

> *(The Deep)* Far down, where the light gives up, something turns its
> attention. You feel it the way a mouse feels the shadow of a wing cross
> the grass — nothing has happened yet. The sea does not get rougher. It
> gets interested.

---

## 7 · The Calypso cash-out — *a temptation, told*

The Act III cash-out: a real ending, offered warmly, never a failure screen.
The frame carries it — the bard makes the stopping honest, and the retelling
wink is load-bearing (other tellings HAVE ended here).

> **Context:** Act III · Ogygia
>
> **Prompt** *(Telling → Frame)*: Seven veils of the world away from Ithaca
> there is an island where nothing has ever gone wrong, and a goddess who
> asks nothing except that you stop rowing. The fig trees bear all year. The
> bed is warm. She would make him deathless, friends, and she means it. —
> Here the telling can end. It has ended here before, at other fires; no one
> who chose the island has ever complained. Or been heard from. Which may be
> the same thing.

- **“Bank the telling here”** ‹told ending, partial Legacy› *(Frame)*: Then
  we leave him there — old friends, warm, unwritten. The fire is yours a
  while yet: I will sing the island, the figs, the seven soft years, and if
  my voice catches once near the end, that is the smoke, and you will not
  ask. ‹ending: The Island That Asks Nothing›
- **“Row on”** *(Frame)*: So he lashes the raft himself, plank by plank,
  because no crew is left to lash it for him. Throw on a branch, friends.
  The sea is not finished, and neither are we.

## 8 · The hollow win — *a standard Ithaca success*

Every standard success ends unreconciled — the prophecy hook is visible from
the player's first win. The frame closes the telling; the last line opens
the next one.

> *(Telling → Frame)* So the bow speaks, the hall is washed, and the queen
> tests him with the bed no man could move — and he answers like a
> carpenter, which is how she knows her husband. That is the ending, friends.
> That is the ending everyone pays for. But I have stood in the doorway of
> this telling and watched him after: a man home, fed, believed — who wakes
> before first light and goes down to stand on the shingle, and does not go
> into the water, and does not go back to bed either. The sea does not
> forget. There is a truer ending than this one. I do not have all of it.
> Yet.

## 9 · The Despair fail — *the burnout, nine fathoms quiet*

The engine's universal fail, reskinned. Deep weather: the fire burns low on
purpose. No thunder — the sea does not defeat him; it outlasts him.

> *(The Deep → Frame)* There is a beach — on Ogygia, on Aeaea, on any of
> them — where a man can sit down with his back to a rock and stop. Not die.
> Stop. The tide fills his footprints and he does not make new ones. — The
> fire is low, friends; that is on purpose. Some men are not lost at sea.
> The sea just outlasts them. We leave him there tonight, looking at the
> water. If you want him to stand up, come back tomorrow, and pay the bard,
> and we will see what can be done.

## 10 · The humor layer — *frame wit, five micro-scenes*

The three comic engines (vanity and fee · the audience's memory · the
retelling wink), each fully in-fiction. The hecklers are canon across all
tellings; their gags accumulate like the prophecy does.

**The retelling wink** *(the roguelike winking at the player)*
> *(Frame)* Last market-day I sang the squall before the lotus, and a man
> from Kyme called me a liar for it. Friend: every telling is true. That is
> what a sea story is — the truth, in a different order each time.

**The prophecy wink** *(the meta-arc, diegetic)*
> *(Frame)* The blind prophet told him the whole road home, every turning of
> it. And I will be honest at this fire: I did not catch all of it the first
> time either. It comes back to me in pieces — one piece a telling. Keep
> coming. Between us we will assemble the man’s fate.

**The potter's boy** *(deadpan devastation — and the stakes kept whole)*
> *(Frame)* The potter’s boy, who has said nothing for an hour, wants to
> know why he didn’t just say sorry to Poseidon. Everyone looks at me. It is
> a fair question, boy. Hold on to it. Ask it again when you have heard what
> the shout cost.

**The bard's vanity** *(rival bards, professional economics)*
> *(Frame)* Phemios of Smyrna sang this in the upper town last month — some
> of you heard him; some of you, I see, are here to compare. He does the
> storm with a drum. A drum! The sea does not need a drum; the sea needs a
> man who has looked at it. Also his Cyclops has two eyes at supper and one
> by the wine, which is the arithmetic of a man who sings flat.

**The horse** *(the running gag)*
> *(Frame)* You want the horse. I can see you wanting the horse. Wrong poem,
> friend — the horse costs extra, and it is carpenters’ work besides.
> Tonight is oars and salt. The horse is another night.

## Micro-register cheat sheet

| Surface | Weather | Length | `!` | Notes |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| Cold open, act intros, recaps | Frame | long | earned, ≤2 | direct address, questions, the hecklers |
| Card prompts | Telling | middle | rare | concrete detail, named crew, no crowd-work |
| Outcomes / results | Telling | middle-long (≤420) | rare | the turn in the last clause |
| Underworld, god-beats, Despair | The Deep | shortening | **zero** | dread is quiet; nothing pulses |
| Choice labels / buttons | Tale-copy | 2–6 words | never | a verb with the door’s flavor |
| Endings | Telling → Frame | long | earned | the frame gets the last word |
| Heckler speech (quoted) | Frame | short | free-ish | exempt from cliché scan; still never `!!` |
