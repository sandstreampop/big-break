# Playtest kit — The Odyssey (Required #5 + product risks 1–3)

One page. Two players minimum, **no explanation** — hand over a phone with
the game open and say only: *"play this; think out loud if you can."* The
kit exists so running the test is costless; the test itself is human work
and cannot be automated. Findings go in the verdict sheet at the bottom;
file completed sheets in `taste-feedback/` alongside the copy verdicts.

**Why this playtest gates everything:** the sims prove a viable probability
distribution, not a game worth mastering (`SIM-FINDINGS.md` names what they
can't see). The review's three product risks — too reverent, predetermined
runs, thin persistent knowledge — are all observation questions. P-B half 2
(landmark variants) and P-C's bard's-note are **built only if this playtest
says so.**

## Primary question (P-C): does anyone come back?

Replay motivation outranks everything else on this page.

- [ ] After run 1 ends, say nothing. Does the player start run 2 unprompted?
- [ ] After run 2? Run 3?
- [ ] When they stop, ask once: *"what would have kept you going?"* — record
      verbatim.
- [ ] Does the prophecy footer ("the bard carries N of three turnings") read
      as a reason to return or as homework? Ask AFTER they notice it
      unprompted — note whether they ever do.
- [ ] If they replay: do they change Fire? Path? Behavior at the name-brag?

## Observation checklist (risks 1–3, watch silently during play)

**Reverence (risk 1):**
- [ ] Do they laugh, ever? At what — note the exact card.
- [ ] Do they read result text to the end, skim it, or skip it? When does
      skimming start (run 1 or run 2)?
- [ ] Any "this is beautiful"-type comment WITHOUT a follow-up run = the
      museum signal. Any "one more" = the counter-signal.

**Predetermination (risk 2):**
- [ ] At the second Cyclops: do they lean in or sigh? Verbatim any comment
      about "same again".
- [ ] Do they notice the itinerary IS fixed? Does that read as structure
      ("the story has shape") or repetition ("I've seen this")?
- [ ] The fifth Cyclops question can't be answered in one sitting — note
      the trend line from encounter 2 → 3.

**Persistent knowledge (risk 3):**
- [ ] Do they understand a fragment was banked when the run ends? (The
      ending copy says so — do they register it?)
- [ ] On a later run: do they recognize the third question when the
      Underworld reroutes? Any visible "oh!"?
- [ ] Does the true-ending hook ("there is a truer ending than this one. I
      do not have all of it. Yet.") land as a hook? Ask at the very end:
      *"the bard said there's a truer ending — do you care?"*

**General flow:**
- [ ] Where do they hesitate longest? (Card + choice — that's where stakes
      are legible.)
- [ ] Do they choose on story or on the visible numbers? (Watch eyes/thumb:
      do they read labels or scan deltas?)
- [ ] Any dead moment where they tap without reading three cards in a row —
      note the act.
