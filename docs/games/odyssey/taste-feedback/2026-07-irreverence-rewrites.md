# Irreverence pass — before/after, for human verdict (P-A, 2026-07)

The review's product risk #1: the pack reads as *"admire this tasteful
adaptation"* rather than *"one more run."* This pass's diagnosis: the deck's
signature lapse is **the museum plaque** — a result that lands on a
detachable aphorism (*"X, friends, is Y"*), often stacked two deep, ending
the card on an abstraction instead of a person. The taste layer itself was
part of the problem: VOICE.md banned "slapstick" outright and kept all
comedy in the frame, which outlawed Homer's own bodily comedy (Elpenor,
Irus, the pigs). The layer is amended (VOICE.md counter-register + law 2/7
clarifications, GUIDING_EXAMPLES § 11) so future generation sessions don't
regress to museum tone; the worst shipped closers are rewritten below.

**A green taste lint proves nothing here — this is precisely where
writer ≠ grader applies.** Verdict each row `love / like / dislike`; recurring
dislikes go back into the blocklist/examples per the feedback loop.

The rewrite bar, from the review: *surprise, cruelty, stupidity, reversal —
from the text.* Land on something a body did.

## Not rewritten, noted for the verdict loop

- Quoted speech keeps its license: Circe's *"The clever survive. The ones who
  make OTHERS clever get home"* is a character speaking, not the bard
  plaquing — left alone.
- Plenty of the deck already carries the counter-register (the sacred cow's
  scorn, "beggars do not drop a man of Iros' tonnage with one measured
  hook", "the party that was also a phalanx", the joke that rows). The tic
  was concentrated in `incredible` closers.
- If the human verdict is that the plaque count is still high, the next
  sweep should audit every `incredible` tier's final sentence — that is
  where the register swells.
