# Bard chatter — voice sample, for verdict (P-A, round 2)

The gap the review and the human both named: the bard’s dry asides ship
inside the cards, but the bard’s *personality* — the frame, the hecklers, the
vanity, the fee, the running gags — lives only in `GUIDING_EXAMPLES.md` and
never reaches a player. This is a sample of the missing layer: short frame
interjections the player would meet between cards and at act breaks.

**Not wired.** Verdict each `love / like / dislike`; the survivors get an
in-game surface (between-card asides / act breaks / both) once the voice is
approved. Register: warm, crowd-working, funny — never mocks the tale, only
himself, his rivals, his audience, his fee. Bar: *surprise, cruelty,
stupidity, reversal — from the text.*

The heckler ensemble (canon, recurring across runs):
**woodpile** = the fact-checker whose grandfather knew a rower ·
**potter’s boy** = deadpan, devastating · **Phemios of Smyrna** = the rival
bard who does the storm with a drum · **the man who wants the horse** = wrong
poem · the bard’s own **vanity and fee**.

## Notes for whoever grades this

- All twelve are frame register (crowd-work), distinct from the telling
  register inside cards — that contrast is the point. They’d surface as short
  interjections, not card prompts.
- The hecklers recur by design: seeing the woodpile correct the count in run
  one and again in run three is the "canon ensemble accumulates" idea, and a
  cheap replay hook.
- Curly quotes/apostrophes throughout, ≤2 `!`, no blocklist clichés — written
  to clear the taste floor so a green lint isn’t the thing standing between
  these and the game; your ear is.
